Urban-Spaces is an ongoing photographic inquiry into the environments that shape everyday movement through the city. Guided by informal paths, this collection observes how urban space is experienced beyond its intended design. This work examines the idea of movement within the built environment. By crossing thresholds, following unofficial routes, or using the city’s own structures in unintended ways, the images suggest what exists beyond barriers, closed gates, and rusted fences. The photographs document not only architecture, but also the quiet freedom of navigating the city on one’s own terms.
